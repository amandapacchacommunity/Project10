Section 1

Public workforce records as a starting point for operational review

This section uses public employee and vacancy data to examine departmental distribution, staffing concentration, and vacancy pressure. It focuses on descriptive structure first, then applies a risk lens to identify where operational exposure may be more visible.

Direct Source Fields Used Here

  • Department name
  • Position title
  • Published salary or hourly rate fields
  • Vacancy counts or staffing-gap records where available

Derived Indicators Used Here

  • Department concentration ratio
  • Vacancy pressure flag
  • Operational watchlist grouping

Interpretation Notes

  • Large departments are not automatically high risk, but they may carry higher operational dependency.
  • Vacancy concentration can indicate staffing strain, hiring lag, or continuity pressure.
  • Use both counts and ratios to avoid overstating risk in high-headcount departments.

Limitation

Workforce counts on this page reflect published public records and do not capture internal management structure, informal reporting lines, or full operational complexity.
