How title inconsistency can affect workforce analysis and pay comparisons

Public administrative data often contains inconsistent naming conventions, title fragmentation, and imperfect alignment with formal classification references. This section shows how those issues can be treated as meaningful risk signals rather than mere cleaning problems.

Suggested Logic

  • Normalize capitalization, punctuation, abbreviations, and obvious title variants.
  • Create a crosswalk from raw title to standardized classification group.
  • Track confidence level for each mapping to document uncertainty.
  • Use standardized groups for salary comparison, not raw titles alone.

Risk Interpretation

When titles are inconsistent, workforce planning, compensation benchmarking, and governance review all become harder. A relatively simple title-cleaning exercise can therefore produce a powerful risk signal: the quality of the classification structure itself.
