Section 4

Adding labor and pay structure context without overstating the data

This section uses public labor agreement references, pay schedule materials, and classification/pay-plan context to interpret compensation structure more realistically. The goal is not to assign every employee to a definitive bargaining unit, but to show how publicly documented labor frameworks can inform workforce analysis.

Suggested Interpretation Standard

  • Use union materials as contextual compensation and labor-structure references.
  • Do not assign every employee to a union unless the public record clearly supports it.
  • Use pay grades and schedules as reference layers, not as a substitute for internal payroll systems.
  • Document where the analysis is direct, inferred, or exploratory.

Why This Matters for Risk Analysis

Compensation structure is shaped by more than raw salaries alone. Union agreements, pay schedules, and grade structures can affect comparability, labor-cost planning, compensation governance, and the realism of workforce analysis. Including that layer makes the project more grounded in how public-sector compensation actually works.

Limitation

Union and pay-grade information on this page is presented as a public reference layer. It should not be read as a complete employee-by-employee bargaining-unit assignment or a definitive internal payroll classification file.
