Public Data Sources

This project uses publicly available datasets and reference materials published by the City of Chicago. Sources include workforce records, vacancy data, classification frameworks, governance materials, and budget documents used as structural context.

Core Workforce Data

  • Current Employee Names, Salaries, and Position Titles
    Base workforce dataset including employee roles, departments, and compensation fields.
  • Workforce Vacancies Dataset
    Department-level vacancy data used to assess staffing gaps and operational pressure.

Classification & Compensation References

  • Classification and Pay Plan
    Official job classification structure used to support title normalization and role grouping.
  • Job Specifications
    Detailed role descriptions used to interpret classification categories and responsibilities.

Governance & Hiring Context

  • Shakman Settlement Materials
    Public documentation related to hiring governance and role classification considerations.
  • City Hiring Plan
    Framework for hiring controls and role designation processes.

2022 Structural Context

  • 2022 Budget Recommendations and Appropriations
    Department-level staffing and funding data used as a proxy for structural analysis.
  • Recovery and Funding Materials
    Public documentation of funding allocations and programmatic expansion signals.

Documentation Notes

  • Datasets and documents are used for illustrative and analytical purposes only.
  • Some sources are structured datasets; others are PDFs or reference materials.
  • Title normalization and classification mapping include documented assumptions.
  • Structural analysis reflects observable public signals, not a complete internal org chart.
