Executive Summary

Using public administrative records to build a realistic workforce risk lens

This project uses publicly available data and documentation to examine workforce composition, title consistency, compensation patterns, governance context, and 2022 structural change signals. The goal is not to reproduce internal city systems, but to show how imperfect public records can still support thoughtful, well-documented analysis.

Project Scope

  • Assess workforce composition using public employee and vacancy records.
  • Standardize titles and evaluate classification consistency.
  • Use governance materials as context for role-status interpretation.
  • Use 2022 budget and related materials as structural evidence, not a complete org chart.

Interpretation Standard

  • Direct dataset fields are reported as published.
  • Standardized role groups are derived and documented.
  • Governance and structural indicators are treated as analytical signals.
  • Where public information is incomplete, uncertainty is stated explicitly.

What This Site Covers

  • Workforce — employee distribution, vacancy pressure, and concentration patterns
  • Classification — title normalization and compensation comparison logic
  • Governance Context — public governance materials and role-status interpretation
  • 2022 Structural Signals — public-health, funding, and digital transformation indicators
  • Methods — assumptions, derived fields, and limitations
  • Sources — public source inventory and documentation notes

Project Framing

This project is intentionally realistic in scope. It does not claim access to internal organizational charts, confidential classification systems, or official city risk ratings. Instead, it demonstrates how a risk analyst can organize public workforce records and reference documents into a transparent, defensible analytical model.

Important Note

Structural findings on this site should be read as public structural signals, not as a complete representation of internal organizational design or formal legal conclusions.
